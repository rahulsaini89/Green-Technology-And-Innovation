Waste Managment

Waste management refers to the collection, transportation, processing, recycling, and disposal of various types of waste materials generated by human activities. The primary goal of waste management is to minimize the adverse environmental and health impacts of waste, promote resource recovery and recycling, and reduce the amount of waste sent to landfills or incinerated. It involves the systematic and organized handling of waste to protect public health, conserve natural resources, and minimize the negative environmental consequences associated with waste generation.

Sustainable Agriculture

Sustainable agriculture is a farming and food production approach that aims to meet the current needs for agricultural products while also ensuring the long-term well-being of the environment, society, and future generations. It is a holistic and integrated approach to farming that seeks to balance economic profitability, environmental stewardship, and social responsibility.

Hydroelectric Power

Hydroelectric power, also known as hydroelectricity or hydropower, is a renewable energy technology that harnesses the energy of flowing or falling water to generate electricity. It is one of the oldest and most widely used sources of renewable energy, with a history dating back to ancient civilizations. Hydroelectric power is considered a clean and sustainable energy source because it produces electricity without direct greenhouse gas emissions and relies on the Earth's natural water cycle.

Solar Energy

Solar energy is a renewable and sustainable source of power that is derived from the radiant energy of the sun. It is harnessed using various technologies to convert sunlight into electricity, heat, or other forms of energy for various practical applications. Solar energy is considered an environmentally friendly and clean energy source because it does not produce greenhouse gas emissions or air pollutants during its generation.

Waste Managment. Solid-waste management,

the collecting, treating, and disposing of solid material that is discarded because it has served its purpose or is no longer useful. Improper disposal of municipal solid waste can create unsanitary conditions, and these conditions in turn can lead to pollution of the environment and to outbreaks of vector-borne disease—that is, diseases spread by rodents and insects. The tasks of solid-waste management present complex technical challenges. They also pose a wide variety of administrative, economic, and social problems that must be managed and solved

Science & Tech. Solar Energy,

radiation from the Sun capable of producing heat, causing chemical reactions, or generating electricity. The total amount of solar energy incident on Earth is vastly in excess of the world’s current and anticipated energy requirements. If suitably harnessed, this highly diffused source has the potential to satisfy all future energy needs. In the 21st century solar energy is expected to become increasingly attractive as a renewable energy source because of its inexhaustible supply and its nonpolluting character, in stark contrast to the finite fossil fuels coal, petroleum, and natural gas..
